What does your predicted grade mean?

Based off of the grip strength and other filters you chose, climbers with the same attributes climb that grade on average. If the grade you climb is higher than the predicted grade, this can mean several things: grip strength may not be your strength, difference in grading, ahead of learning curve, etc. On the other hand if your actual grade is lower, you might not be climbing to your potential, your gym is harder, etc. Your predicted grade is ultimately an indicator of where you lie amongst the climbers surveyed in Hangar 18 gyms. For more info on the study, please feel free to read the "Climbing Study" page!

Note: Calculator does not do a great job calculating grades v9+ due to lack of data of v9+ climbers.

Summary of Climber's Data

Goal of Climbing Study Hypothesis

The main goal is to answer the question, is there a correlation between grip strength and the grade people climb? My hypothesis is yes. However, anyone would know techinqiue is just as important as strength when climbing. Could we also make a correlation between experience as a metric of technique, grip strength, and the grade people climb? I thought yes again. While seeking to answer these questions, other interesting questions like is there a difference between how much grip strength and experience affect male and female climbers? Is there a difference between the three Hangar 18 gyms? I would guess male climbers would be more reliant on strength while female climbers on techinque. Likewise, I struggle at Orange Hangar 18 opposed to Rancho Upland making me think Orange might be "hardest". Read the Climbing Study to find the results of the Study!

Future Climbing Study Finger strength

The journey of me seeing Alex Honnold squeeze the hand dynamometer on Youtube to me launching an independent climbing study in my free time has been a fun journey. I had the pleasure of meeting and familiarizing myself with many cool people at my local climbing gyms as well as finding out interesting insight on climbing. My curiousity has only sparked more as now that I see the correlation between grip strength and climbing, I want to know the correlation between finger strength and climbing as I feel finger strength is significantly more important than grip strength. This however would require much more setup opposed to taking a small device off of Amazon. I hope to one day drag tons of weights and a harness to gyms to start this new climbing study!
